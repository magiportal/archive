Kay Kwan

Motion designer and video editor specialising in vibrant, cartoonish 3D animation and 2D motion graphics.

Kay Kwan is a Melbourne-based motion designer and video editor specialising in vibrant, cartoonish 3D animation and 2D motion graphics, holding a Master of Animation, Games & Interactivity from RMIT University. Drawing on a background in film studies and advertising design, Kay has worked across advertising agencies, freelance projects and in-house roles, combining technical expertise with expressive storytelling. Their 360-degree animation Coffin Room has been recognised at festivals internationally, and their earlier work An Awakening Adventure has screened across Australia, Canada, the United States, Portugal, the United Kingdom and Germany.

Nhu Bui

Associate Lecturer (MAGI) and practice-based researcher designing 3D digital heritage experiences through XR.

Nhu Bui is an Associate Lecturer in the Master of Animation, Games & Interactivity (MAGI) Program and a PhD Candidate in Design at RMIT University. Her research sits at the intersection of extended reality (XR), digital heritage, sensory ethnography, and community-engaged design. Growing up in Saigon, Vietnam and now based in Naarm (Melbourne), she is driven by a commitment to safeguarding intangible cultural heritage, namely everyday practices, memories, gestures, atmospheres, and craft-based knowledge, through innovative 3D, AR/VR, and interactive media methods.
